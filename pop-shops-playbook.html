01
Program Philosophy

This section aligns all City of Bothell staff around the intent of the Pop Shops program. It prevents the program from drifting into a standard landlord-tenant relationship over time. Staff should return to these principles regularly — especially as the program grows and new team members join.

"The long-term sustainability of Pop Shops depends less on the physical spaces themselves and more on whether the city creates repeatable operational systems that survive beyond individual staff members or vendor cohorts."
Core principles
  • The goal is experimentation, not guaranteed permanent retail. Both expanding into a storefront and returning to online-only with lessons learned are successful outcomes.
  • Staff should act as facilitators and connectors — not retail managers or compliance officers.
  • The city's role is to reduce friction and encourage learning, not to evaluate business viability.
  • Consistency matters more than perfection. A stable, predictable program builds trust with vendors and the community.
  • Ask "are they learning?" before asking "are they profitable?" — especially in the first 12 months.
  • Program operations must be documented so they are not dependent on any single staff member.
What success looks like

Graduated to storefront

Vendor used Pop Shops as a launchpad and signed a commercial lease in Bothell or nearby.

Returned with new knowledge

Vendor returned to online-only with clearer product-market fit and stronger operations.

Community connection built

Vendor built a local customer base and partnerships with other Bothell businesses.

Cohort contributed to next

Vendor shared learnings or mentored an incoming Pop Shops participant.

02
Vendor Lifecycle Playbook

The operational heartbeat of Pop Shops. Follow these steps for every vendor, every cycle. Each stage has a checklist, script, or framework to guide staff through the process consistently.

Pre-arrival checklist

Complete all items before the vendor's first day.

  • Space cleaned, inspected, and ready for occupancy
  • Welcome kit assembled and placed in the space
  • QR codes for city resources tested and working
  • Vendor added to Pop Shops directory and social media schedule
  • Emergency and after-hours contacts confirmed with vendor
  • Access, key, and logistics walkthrough scheduled
  • Vendor record created in tracking system
  • Welcome email sent (see Section 5 for template)
  • 30-day check-in date calendared
30-day check-in guide

This can be a brief email or a 15-minute call. The goal is to catch friction early — not to evaluate performance.

Questions to ask
  • Any operational friction or confusion since move-in?
  • Are customers finding the space okay?
  • Any infrastructure or facility concerns?
  • What's one thing the city could do to support you right now?
Escalation guide — if vendor says this, do this
Foot traffic is lowOffer a collaborative social media feature post. Check if signage is adequate.
Setup feels confusingSchedule a 30-minute in-person walkthrough with city staff.
I'm overwhelmedConnect with the Bothell SBDC or a peer vendor mentor.
Hours are inconsistentRevisit expectations. Flag in tracker as Yellow if recurring.
Everything is greatLog as Green. Ask if they'd like to be featured in city communications.
6-month review framework

This is a strategic conversation. Focus on learning, not profit. Send the survey 1 week before the meeting.

Survey questions
  • What has changed in your business since opening?
  • What have you learned about your customers?
  • What experiments have you tried? What worked?
  • What support gaps exist that the city could address?
  • Are you on track toward your original goals?
  • What would you tell a new Pop Shops vendor?
Staff tracking notes after review
  • Update vendor health status (Green / Yellow / Red)
  • Log key learnings in tracking system
  • Note any resource referrals made
  • Calendar 18-month transition planning date
18-month transition planning guide

Help the vendor land intentionally — whether they're growing, pivoting, or graduating from the program.

Step 1Hold a transition conversation using the off-ramp discussion template (see Section 5).
Step 2Assess path: storefront expansion, return to online, pivot, or program extension request.
Step 3Provide the Bothell Commercial Corridor Resource Sheet (local brokers, permitting contacts).
Step 4Connect to relevant resources: SBDC, financing programs, business licensing support.
Step 5Log final outcome in the annual program review dashboard.
03
Vendor Health Monitoring

Proactive program management. Review all vendor statuses monthly and update the tracker accordingly. The goal is to intervene early and supportively — not punitively.

Green — On Track
Consistent posted hours
Active merchandising
Responsive to city outreach
Engaged on social/online
Positive customer feedback
Yellow — Watch
Inconsistent presence
Declining engagement
Minimal updates or changes
Operational frustration signs
Slow response to outreach
Red — At Risk
Repeated unannounced closures
Signs of abandonment
Non-responsive to city
Clear disengagement
Customer complaints received
Response protocols
 Green response
Log status in tracker. Offer optional spotlight feature in city communications. Invite to share learnings with incoming vendors if interested.
 Yellow response
Send a supportive check-in message — not a warning. Offer promotional assistance or a walkthrough visit. Consider connecting with a peer vendor or SBDC resource. Re-assess in 30 days.
 Red response
Initiate a formal conversation within 5 business days. Determine vendor intent to continue. Co-create either a recovery plan with clear milestones or a structured, supportive exit plan. Document all steps taken.

Recommended tools

Any of the following work well for a program of this size: Google Sheets (free, collaborative, easy to share), Airtable (structured, good for filtered views and forms), or Notion (flexible, works well for combining notes and data). 06
Annual Program Review

At the end of each program year, produce an Annual Review Packet. This justifies future funding, supports grant applications, and communicates program impact to City Council and community stakeholders.

Annual review checklist
  • Vendor outcomes summary (how many graduated, pivoted, or exited)
  • Occupancy rate and vacancy trends over the year
  • Aggregate vendor self-reported growth indicators
  • Lessons learned — what worked, what didn't
  • Recommended policy or process changes for next year
  • Economic activation observations (foot traffic, events held, community impact)
  • Staff time and resource usage summary
  • Vendor testimonials collected (at least 2)
Suggested report sections for City Council
OverviewProgram purpose, number of vendors served, and spaces operated this year
OutcomesVendor trajectory data — graduated, returned to online, pivoted, still active
LearningsWhat the program discovered about supporting small businesses in Bothell
Community impactEvents, media mentions, foot traffic data, partner organization engagements
Year aheadProposed improvements, resource requests, and recommended policy changes
