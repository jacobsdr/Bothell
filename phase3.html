Phase 3 of 3

Decide

Months 19–24 of your two-year program

You've spent nearly two years running a real experiment. Now it's time to look honestly at what you've learned and decide what comes next — with evidence, not guesswork.

The core principle of Phase 3

There is no wrong answer here. The point of this entire program was to help you find out whether physical retail fits your business. If the answer is yes — great, let's help you take the next step. If the answer is no — that's equally valuable. You saved yourself years of expensive leases and found out on the city's dime instead of your own.

Five honest questions to answer before you decide

Does the revenue justify the time?

Add up what you've made across your weekends. Subtract the cost of any materials, fees, or time away from your online business. Is the net positive enough to build on — and would a permanent space make that math better or worse?

Did you find a customer base you couldn't reach online?

Some businesses discover a completely different type of customer in person — often older, or more local, or more impulse-driven than their online audience. If physical retail unlocked a new market, that's a strong signal.

Did you enjoy it?

Don't underestimate this. Running a permanent physical store is a lifestyle choice, not just a business decision. Four days a week of customer interaction is very different from running an Etsy shop in your spare time. Be honest about whether you actually liked it.

Did your online business benefit or suffer?

Some vendors find the pop-up drives more online sales through word of mouth and social content. Others find the time commitment pulled them away from their core business. Which was true for you?

What would you need to change to make permanent retail work?

If your answer to the first four questions is "mostly yes but..." — what is the "but"? More foot traffic? A different location? Lower overhead? A partner to share shifts? Sometimes the issue is solvable, which means the answer might still be yes.

Your two paths forward

Physical retail worked

Ready to grow

  • Contact the city — they can serve as a reference for landlords in Bothell
  • Connect with the Small Business Development Center (SBDC) for free lease negotiation coaching
  • Explore Bothell's Main Street commercial corridor for available spaces
  • Use your two years of sales data as your business case — it's more credible than projections
  • Ask Mercy Corps Northwest about small business loans for build-out costs

Online is the right fit

Taking what you learned

  • Your bestselling in-person items deserve more prominence online — feature them
  • The customer questions you heard in person are your best FAQ and product description copy
  • The social content you created for your pop-up can keep working for your online brand
  • Consider using weekend markets as a lower-commitment physical presence going forward
  • You now know your customer in a way that most online-only sellers never do — use that
